Mount Everest Base Camps

There are two base camps on Mount Everest on opposite sides of the mountains. The South Base Camp is in Nepal at an altitude of 17,598 ft (5,364 meters), while North Base Camp is in China at 16,900 ft (5,150 meters). The base camps are simple campsites at the base of Mount Everest that are used by mountain climbers during their ascent and descent. Climbers typically rest at base camp for several days for acclimatization to reduce the risk of altitude sickness.

South Base Camp Trek

Every year, about 40,000 people trek to the South Base Camp. So, while you might think of the summit when you think about trekkers, very few actually do. Only 4,000 people have successfully summited Everest, with around 800 people a year even attempting it.
